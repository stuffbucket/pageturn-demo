multi-angle pivot capture

Replay of harness/scenarios/multi-angle-pivot.json (a vertical-biased drag from the top-right corner) seen from 8 camera presets at 7 dihedral checkpoints.

What to look for in each angle:

  • front — Default head-on. Best for back-face bleed and cover gradient continuity. Hides pivot defects and z-axis drift.
  • top — Top-down. Spine is a vertical line; off-spine pivot bugs (#68/#76) are immediate.
  • side-spine — Spine-edge view. Curl-into-tube shows as a coil; z-axis drift during settle is visible.
  • side-corner — Down the drag-axis. Shows whether dihedral aligns with drag direction.
  • three-quarter — 30/45 thumbnail angle. Useful default for evidence captures.
  • worm — From below. Reveals back-face issues and missing n+1 leaf (#54/#58).
  • behind — From behind the spine. Confirms back-face texture sampling and mirror-flip orientation.
  • iso — Isometric. Equal-axis foreshortening — best for cross-run side-by-side comparisons.
